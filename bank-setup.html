Bank setup

Fidelity Investments

By linking an external bank account to their Fidelity account, customers are able to transfer money in and out of Fidelity easily. This process saves the customer time as they won’t have to visit a branch to deposit a check.

EFT processing was a Top 10 support call driver within Fidelity’s Personal Investing space in 2017, which resulted in 850,000 calls annually. 40% of those calls were customers who weren't aware that there were different ways to move money.

While the process of adding a bank to Fidelity isn’t new, the current experience sat in a 300px wide modal and didn’t provide customers with the confidence they needed to feel like they were linking their bank successfully. The modal had no additional real estate, therefore didn’t provide proper inline context and didn’t empower the customer to recover when presented with system errors.

Process

In order to increase customer confidence, we redesigned a full page experience where the process of adding a bank was consistent across transfer methods. We also introduced new functionality: the ability to “copy” (link their existing external bank to other Fidelity accounts with one click) their bank instructions and the ability to upload their bank documents if Fidelity is unable to immediately verify their external bank’s information.

Results

In the first 4 months since release, the new Standalone Add a Bank app reduced support calls by 110,000 and increased transaction completion rate to 86%. 135,000 new banks were added and as a result of improving the digital experience, our monthly average NIGO (not-in-good-order) rate decreased by 25% and monthly average mailed-in forms decreased by 30%.

By allowing customers to “copy” their banks, we saw a 200% increase in banks copied as it saved customer’s time when they wanted to transfer money as they wouldn’t have to repeat the process with each individual account.
